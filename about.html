Project Overview

In the modern on-demand food delivery landscape, success depends on operational efficiency and matching restaurant offerings with customer expectations. The Food Ordering Behaviour and Customer Trends project was initiated to synthesize delivery logistics and customer feedback.

By leveraging transactions data, customer ratings, and geographic points, we constructed a multi-layered analysis that uncovers key friction points in ordering platforms. The project traces raw data through cleaning stages in Python (Pandas/NumPy), database modeling via SQL, and final interactive representation using Tableau.

Primary Analytics Goal: To convert transactional datasets into actionable restaurant profiles, driver fleet evaluations, and demographic segmentation models.

Business Problem

Platform management groups face three key operational concerns:

Logistical Delays: Delivery times frequently spike during periods of poor weather and peak times. We need to identify variables contributing to dispatch bottlenecks.
Rating Divergences: Certain cuisines receive low customer ratings despite high order volumes, suggesting product quality issues or delivery-time sensitivities.
Customer Retention: Order sizes and average spending values fluctuate significantly between student demographics and standard family profiles.

Visualizations Used

The Tableau dashboard uses several specific visualization components to clarify findings:

Choropleth Delivery Maps: Color-scales regions by average courier speed to isolate urban gridlock zones.
Scatter Plot Correlation Matrices: Maps average customer rating against average preparation time to test if prep times drive rating outcomes.
Dual-Axis Trend Lines: Tracks hour-by-hour order volumes alongside courier capacities to identify driver deficits.

Business Insights

Synthesizing the query outputs and Tableau dashboards yielded several key operational findings:

1. Peak-Hour Deliveries & Weather Spikes: Delivery times increase by up to 35% during rainy weather. This delay is mainly caused by lack of courier coverage rather than prep times. Dynamic routing adjustments for bad weather could help stabilize delivery times.

2. Cuisine Margin Paradox: While Fast Food commands the highest total order count (representing 45% of total order volume), its AOV is lower ($12.50) compared to Continental and Asian food types ($32.40). Restaurants should bundle drinks or desserts to increase low AOVs.

3. The Preparedness Deficit: Orders placed between 7 PM and 9 PM experience a 12-minute prep delay compared to afternoon periods. This indicates restaurant kitchen capacity bottlenecks during peak dinner times.

Conclusion & Recommendations

Based on the data trends, we recommend the following strategies to improve operations:

Implement Weather-Induced Incentives: Introduce weather-based bonuses for couriers during rainy/stormy periods to ensure adequate driver supply and prevent order backlog.
Introduce Smart Bundling Promos: Drive higher basket values by automatically suggesting beverage and side pairings for restaurant categories with lower AOVs (under $15.00).
Promote Kitchen Staggering Rules: Offer minor customer discounts for ordering outside of peak hours (e.g. 6:00 PM or 9:15 PM) to reduce pressure on restaurant kitchens.
